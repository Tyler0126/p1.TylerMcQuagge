Issues With Current Design

There are a couple of issues with the current design of this microwave. This first issue I can think of is that whenever you press the Time Cook button, it displays a message in the digital screen that says TIME. Whenever you also press the Time On/Off button, this same message is displayed on the digital screen of the microwave. Having both of these buttons display the same exact message even though they perform completely different functions could be a little confusing.

There is also the issue of the Popcorn, Potato, Beverage, and Reheat buttons at the top of the microwave as shown in the image below.

These buttons are featured at the top of the microwave which makes them seem like they should be really important and frequently used buttons, but I hardly ever use these buttons at all. I feel like it may just be personal to me, but this seems like a big design flaw that there are buttons that I hardly ever use and they are at the very top of the control interface.

One more design flaw that has caused issues for me is the location of the clock and AM/PM buttons pictured below.

The clock button works by letting the user select the time on the microwave once the clock button is pressed. When the user selects the clock button, they can input a time using the numbers and then they are required to enter if it is AM or PM using the AM/PM button. The first time I manually set the time on my microwave, I was confused as to where the AM/PM button was because it was no where near the clock button. This does not make sense to me, and I feel like the Clock and AM/PM buttons should be right next to eachother in the control interface.

Common and Rare Uses

There are a couple of uses of the microwave that I use way more often than other times. One of the most common ways I use this microwave is by manually selecting the Time Cook button and then inputting the exact time I want to cook my food for. The second most common way I use my microwave is whenever I am cooking food in my oven, I will utilize the timer on the microwave to keep track of how long I need to cook my food.

Some of the rarer ways I use this microwave is to defrost frozen chicken before I cook with it, setting the time on the microwave whenever daylight savings happens or the power goes out, and to cook popcorn in the microwave.

I believe this control interface makes doing the most common things pretty easy and intuitive, and I have not had any issues with the tasks of the microwave I use the most. The most common sequence of action is that I walk up to the microwave, open up the door, and put my food in and close the door. Then I will push Time Cook and manually enter the amount of time I want my food to cook using the number buttons on the control interface. Once I have my time inputted in, I click the Start button to begin cooking my food.

The microwave provides feedback to the user in a couple different ways. When the user selects a button, it shows an appropriate message on the digital portion of the control interface corresponding to the button that was pressed. Also, whenever the timer has couunted down to zero, the microwave makes a beeping sound to let the user know that the time has expired and the food is done cooking.

Some common mistakes I can make with the current design of the microwave is going up and immediatley typing in the amount of time I want the food to cook in the microwave before pressing the Time Cook button. Doing this just immediately implements the express cook feature and starts cooking my food for however many minutes based on which number I pressed on the control interface. Another common mistake I make when using the microwave's control interface is that I will sometimes mean to press Time Cook to heat my food up and I will accidentally press the Timer button just because they are similarly labeled. This usually only happens whenever I am in a rush and am not paying that good of attention.
